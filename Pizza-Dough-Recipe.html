How to Make Pizza Dough

Ingredients

  • 2 1/4 cups plain flour (plus extra for dusting)
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1 tbsp olive oil (optional)
  • 1 tsp active dry yeast
  • 3/4 cup warm water (around 110°F/45°C)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil (for greasing)

Instructions

Activate the yeast

In a small bowl, mix the warm water, sugar, and active dry yeast. Stir until the yeast dissolves and let it sit for about 5-10 minutes until it becomes frothy. If it doesn’t bubble, the yeast might be expired, so try again with fresh yeast.

Make the dough

In a large bowl, combine the flour and salt. Once the yeast mixture is frothy, pour it into the bowl with the flour. Add the olive oil (if using). Stir with a spoon or your hands until the dough begins to come together.

Transfer the dough to a lightly floured surface and knead for about 8-10 minutes until smooth and elastic. If the dough is sticky, add a little more flour, a tablespoon at a time.

Let the dough rise

Grease a clean bowl with a little olive oil and place the dough in it, turning the dough once to coat it in oil. Cover the bowl with a damp towel or plastic wrap and let it rise in a warm place for about 1-1.5 hours, or until it has doubled in size.

Shape the dough

Once the dough has risen, punch it down gently to release any air bubbles. Divide the dough into 2 portions for smaller pizzas or keep it as one large portion for a bigger pizza. Roll out the dough on a floured surface until it reaches your desired thickness (about 10-12 inches in diameter for a standard pizza).

Preheat the oven

Preheat your oven to its highest setting, around 475°F (245°C), for at least 30 minutes. If you have a pizza stone, place it in the oven while preheating.

Assemble the pizza

Transfer the rolled-out dough onto a parchment paper or lightly floured pizza peel. Add your desired toppings and bake for about 10-15 minutes, or until the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbly.

Serve

Slice, serve, and enjoy!
